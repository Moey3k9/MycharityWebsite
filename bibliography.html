What is virtual reality in tourism?

Virtual reality in tourism is a topic that accelerates. With virtual reality, it is very important to stay up to date and have basic knowledge of technology if you plan on joining this fast-paced industry. Virtual tour is the simulation of an existing location with the help of sequential videos or still images. Other multimedia foundations such as floor plans, sound effects and music can also be used as they can be recreating a much more realistic representation of reality.
Virtual tours help people who don’t have time as it’s an excellent alternative to fieldwork and it can also save a lot of money.
A 360-degree image is a controllable panoramic photo which surrounds the original point from which the shot was taken. The photos are then lined up to make a continuous circle around the point of the shooting. Digital cameras have built in software that helps align the shots.
A 360-degree videos also known as immersive videos is when a video recording shows a view from every direction. This means that they all have been recorded at the same time using an omnidirectional camera or a collection of cameras. This then allows you to look around in all directions just as they can in real life. As VR technology becomes more common. Video quality helps to make a good user experience.
It’s important to understand that screen resolution for 360 videos is different than standard screen resolutions.
